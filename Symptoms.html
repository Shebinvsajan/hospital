Piles/Hemorrhoids

Piles, also known as hemorrhoids, are swollen veins in the rectum and anus. They can be a painful and uncomfortable condition, but they are also very common.

  • Internal hemorrhoids: These develop inside the rectum and are usually painless. You may not even know you have them.
  • External hemorrhoids: These develop under the skin around the anus and can be painful, itchy, and bleed.

Take a Self Check

  • Pain or discomfort in the anus
  • Itching or burning around the anus
  • Bleeding during bowel movements
  • A lump near the anus
  • Feeling like you need to poo even after you have been to the toilet

Causes of piles

  • Straining during bowel movements
  • Sitting on the toilet for long periods
  • Pregnancy
  • ADiarrhea or constipation
  • Obesity
  • Lifting heavy objects

What are Anal Fissures?

An anal fissure is a tear or crack in the skin lining the anal canal, the last part of the digestive tract. This tear can be shallow or deeper, extending into the underlying muscle.

Common Causes of Anal Fissures

  • Constipation and straining during bowel movements
  • Passing large or hard stools
  • Diarrhea
  • Childbirth
  • Inflammatory bowel disease

Symptoms of Anal Fissures

  • Pain
  • Bleeding
  • Itching.
  • A visible tear

Preventing Anal Fissures

  • Maintain regular bowel movements
  • Eat a high-fiber diet straining.
  • Drink plenty of fluids
  • Avoid straining
  • Wipe gently

Pilonidal sinus

An anal fissure is a tear or crack in the skin lining the anal canal, the last part of the digestive tract. This tear can be shallow or deeper, extending into the underlying muscle.

Causes

  • Ingrown hairs are the primary cause of pilonidal sinus. These hairs can pierce the skin, causing an inflammatory response and forming a cyst or abscess.
  • Friction and pressure from tight clothing or sitting for long periods can contribute to the development of pilonidal sinus.
  • Family history and obesity are also considered risk factors.

Symptoms

  • A small pit or bump in the gluteal cleft
  • Pain, especially when sitting or moving
  • Redness, swelling, and warmth
  • Drainage of pus or blood
  • Fever (in case of an abscess)

Preventing Pilonidal Sinus

  • Hair removal
  • Maintaining good hygiene
  • Avoid tight clothing
  • Maintain a healthy weight

Varicose veins

Varicose veins are swollen, twisted veins that can occur anywhere in the body, but are most common in the legs. They usually develop when the valves in the veins become weak or damaged, allowing blood to pool and the veins to bulge.

Symptoms of varicose veins

  • Bulging, bluish veins
  • Aching or heaviness in the legs
  • Fatigue
  • Itching or burning around the veins
  • Muscle cramps
  • Swelling in the ankles and feet
  • Skin changes, such as darkening or dryness

Causes of Varicose veins

  • Family history
  • Age
  • Pregnancy
  • Hormonal changes
  • Obesity
  • Standing or sitting for long periods of time
  • Wearing tight clothing

Hidradenitis suppurativa

Hidradenitis suppurativa (HS), also known as acne inversa, is a chronic inflammatory skin disease that affects the apocrine sweat glands. These glands are primarily found in areas with hair follicles, such as the armpits, groin, genital area, and under the breasts.

Symptoms

  • Painful bumps or nodules under the skin
  • Blackheads or whiteheads
  • Abscesses that may drain pus or blood
  • Scarring
  • Tunneling under the skin
  • Fatigue and pain that can affect daily activities
